# NostOS — full version history

Every version, newest first, one line each. This is the complete record; the
README carries only the recent window and the milestones and links here.

Design detail and planning live in [PAI-version-plan.md](PAI-version-plan.md);
what is still ahead lives in [docs/ROADMAP.md](docs/ROADMAP.md).

| Version | Summary |
|---|---|
| v1.150 | **The dashboard drops your name.** It was costing a line of the wide HUD to tell you something you cannot act on: your name never changes, and you already know it. The status lines and the score are what the dashboard is for. It still appears where it earns its place — the title screen, the SMS threads, and the death certificate. |
| v1.149 | **A HUD pass, and the panels stop being painted over.** Icons at last for the two items that were falling through to the icon switch's default case — a plain coloured square: the starting **folded note** is now a sheet folded once with the crease and the ghost of handwriting, and **moly** is drawn as Homer has it, black root and milk-white flower (Od. X.304-306). The status lines read `ISLAND:` / `AI:` in caps with the names in Sentence case, right-justified, one font and one tone; the **score** shrinks and moves to its own line above the slot row with a tiny SCORE beside it — stacked in the corner it had been sitting on top of the PHONE slot on a narrow screen. **Tap an SMS** and it goes: the ttl is pulled down to the tail of its own fade, so it dims out in a beat rather than vanishing mid-sentence. The **Record panel** is rebuilt — sized to its content instead of a fixed height with a hole in the middle of an early run, fully responsive so it shrinks to a handset (332x306 at 360 wide) rather than keeping desktop metrics and overflowing, PRACTICE collapsed to one line, felled obelisks **bracketed** as `[OB-1A2B]`, and the four daemons drawn as actual silicon: DIP packages with legs down both edges, a pin-1 notch, and the name silkscreened across the back, struck through as each falls. And the world's overlays no longer paint over an open panel: the touch buttons, toasts and occlusion ghost all drew after the panels, so a JUMP button sat on the last chip. Suppressed now — and cleared from the hit lists too, since not drawn must also mean not tappable. Fixes a facing bug from the strait: it hardcoded the hull's mirror, so a ship that had sailed west was drawn facing east. |
| v1.148 | **The HUD says where you are, and the deadline learns to speak.** In a five-island game the dashboard never said which island you were standing on or whose machines you were fighting. It does now: two right-justified grey lines, `Island:` and `AI:`, in one font throughout — same family, size and weight for label and value alike, differing only in tone — and unboxed — this is reference, to be glanced at, not instrumentation to be watched. A felled daemon is struck through rather than recoloured, so the state reads without spending colour. The **score** is pinned hard into the bottom-right corner of the panel as the one coloured thing on it. The **POSEIDON countdown is gone from the HUD**: a number ticking in the corner is wallpaper inside a minute. It arrives as escalating texts instead — automated pre-activation notices from a system scheduling its own waking, in the same flat corporate register as the roaming welcomes, running down from *No action is required of you. No action is available to you.* through *You are advised to be elsewhere. There is no elsewhere.* to a bare **ONE HOUR.** Network-wide, so they reach you on every island, and they do not replay on reload. **Rank leaves the HUD too**, into a new **RECORD** block on the skills panel (**K**): score, rank, AI disabled, islands reached, OBs downed, deaths, and the deadline to look up. Also: the touch **JUMP/RUN** labels are centred properly — glyph and word balanced about the middle rather than the word hung off the rim — with a dark halo so they read over sand and graffiti; and Ogygia's roaming welcome loses its stuttered second *Enjoy your stay* and opens in her own voice, **Welcome to my island.** |
| v1.147 | **Bug fix: each island now saves its own world.** The run snapshot was written when the game had one island and quietly became wrong when the archipelago landed: `obDown` and `boxesOpened` only ever read CALYPSO's arrays, so felling obelisks or looting caches on Polyphemus, Circe or Helios **saved nothing** — reload and every tower was standing again. Worse, the `fortress` blob serialised whichever island you were on but was restored during module evaluation, when the `fortress` alias still points at Calypso's — so opening Polyphemus's Lion's Gate and reloading wrote that state onto **her** fortress. World state is now keyed by island id and re-applied to each world at the moment it is lazily built, which is also the fix's own trap: the boot score-wipe save fires before the far islands exist, so unbuilt islands are carried forward from the save rather than dropped. Pre-v1.147 saves are folded into the new shape under Calypso and migrate forward on first load. Also: the gate reads **beta** rather than alpha. |
| v1.146 | **The docs catch up with the game.** The README had been left at v1.92 while the build ran on to v1.145 — it still called the phone unbuilt and named the islands APOLLO/ATHENA/HADES, a roster superseded long ago. Rewritten around the archipelago, the sea and the phone, with the win condition corrected (fell all four martial daemons, leave Calypso rather than kill her, then come home to Ithaca) and 53 missing version rows restored. The in-game help gains a **The sea** tab: Ogygia and getting off it, sailing and the chart, the five islands, the strait, the phone, the Backspace road, and coming home — the whole second half of the game, which the help had never mentioned once. `docs/ROADMAP.md` reconciled against the code. |
| v1.145 | **Scylla and Charybdis — the strait that makes you choose.** The first of the sea's own monsters: a hazard that lives *between* islands, on the AEAEA↔THRINACIA passage where Homer puts it (Od. XII). Homer's bargain works because Odysseus has a crew to lose; sailing alone, the shape is kept instead — a certain, bounded loss against an unbounded gamble. **Scylla** takes three stacks off the deck and always lets you through: she reaches pockets and pack storage only, never your hands, and never the **AI card** in any of its three states (no reprint exists, so that exclusion is a tested invariant against a soft-lock). **Charybdis** is rolled — usually a mauling and through, about a 30% chance of being swallowed, the boat going down and you thrown back at the island you sailed from. The strait maims but never kills. Make landfall on Aeaea and **Circe** texts you the advice that is hers in the poem: hug the rock and grieve for the few. Never been there, and you choose blind. |
| v1.144 | **The mountain gets its weather.** Mist ramped hard — temple banks 7→12, summit 9→16, with a high breath floor so it stays a solid cloud instead of thinning to wisps; the summit cloud now spills *past* the white snow-cap onto the darker slopes (pale vapour over white snow barely read) and billows above the peak, so it crowns the mountain. The **snow-cap** is patchy rather than a solid block: a core disc at the summit centre, snow chance falling off with distance and height, fraying into the rock on the lower slopes. A snowy peak, not a wizard's hat. |
| v1.143 | **Landfall now counts as a checkpoint.** First arrival on AEGILIA, AEAEA, THRINACIA or ITHACA writes a Load-list checkpoint (+15 each), so a death resumes from the shore you reached. The **lyre** console gains `time day|night|0-23` to jump the clock and exercise the light ramp, ambience and torch veil on demand — both presets sit after the 09:00 start so the toggle never rolls the day forward into POSEIDON's deadline. Fix: leaving an island by boat stamped the mid-sea row-out coordinate as that island's return position, so sailing back dropped you in the water. A sea crossing now lands at the destination's spawn and clears the departed island's return position, so both ends re-beach. |
| v1.142 | **The camera climbs the mountain with you.** It tracks the ground height you stand on, so the sprite stays centred on the ascent instead of walking off the top of the view; the torch's night-veil and sight cone anchor to the sprite's actual on-screen pixel, matching the lift. Water is **grounded** — rivers and streams are forced to height 0 with their banks relaxed down, so they no longer perch above the terrain. Summit and temple mist tightened (more banks, denser puffs, smaller radius). And the **phone** now connects to whichever daemon rules the island you are on — POLYPHEMUS, CIRCE and HELIOS answer their own tab. |
| v1.141 | **A mountain rises on Aegilia.** One great peak far above the ordinary hills (which cap at 8), stamped with a linear descent so the one-step climb clamp never shaves the summit: bare stone above the tree line, a **snow** cap near the top, an alpine conifer fringe below the rock, and trees terracing up the flanks. SMS messages get a *Sender · HH:MM* header off the in-world clock with a hairline rule, so one sender's texts read apart from the next. Land anywhere and the dead carrier pushes a **roaming welcome** in the flat voice of a company with no customers left, only subjects — Aegilia's reads "Coverage is a single cell. It has already seen you." The five palettes are pushed apart on chroma as well as hue: Aegilia greyed to rock, Aeaea a dark over-fed green, Thrinacia bleached to dry gold, Ithaca the warmest. **Crash fix** (a v1.139 regression): the new voyage-state guard in `persist()` read three variables declared far below it with `let`, while `persist` is called during module evaluation — so any existing save hit the temporal dead zone and threw before boot. Black screen, Continue broken. The v1.139 check passed only because it was tested on a fresh game, where that call site never runs. |
| v1.140 | **Sacred mist over the healing groves.** The old marble temples knit your wounds faster, but nothing showed it. A low, slow swirl of pale vapour now lies over each grove — six soft banks orbiting the centre on a gyre and breathing out of phase, drawn flat on the floor pass so the fallen columns and the player stand up out of it. A benign counterpart to the sea's angry fog. |
| v1.139 | **Bug fix: reloads no longer maroon you on the open sea.** Two failures compounded — the 8-second autosave fired regardless of state, so anyone aboard or mid-voyage had their open-water coordinates written to the save, and Continue then dropped them there: a lone figure on the deep with no boat and no way back. `persist()` now skips any transient voyage state, and boot carries a rescue that spirals outward from a restored sea tile to the nearest walkable land, with a line explaining it. |
| v1.138 | **Bug fix: the black wedge in the sea at the shoreline.** The dark triangle that occasionally bit into the water at a beach was raw canvas, not a fill. A carved hollow (or Thrinacia's sandpit) could leave land at height −1 or −2 beside a sea tile pinned flat at 0, and the floor pass returns early for sea — before the skirt is drawn — so nothing painted the vertical face down to the lower ground. Ithaca had nine such faces on the test seed; Calypso and Helios had none, which is why it looked occasional. Now no ground may sit below sea level at the shoreline (lifted, then relaxed outward so no step exceeds one), and a sea tile paints a downward skirt in its own colour as belt-and-braces. |
| v1.137 | **Bug fix: marooned aboard after a crossing.** A regression from the row-out: arriving at any island by ship left you frozen in the boat, unable to step out or board again. The order in the update loop was the whole story — the pending-crossing block ran first and returned without ending the row-out, so the next frame the row-out hijacked the loop with the *previous* island's coordinates and, its own self-clear never firing, returned every frame before any input was read. The row-out now ends where the crossing is committed, not where the voyage hopes to notice. Also: "Press H for help" retires at 40 seconds instead of 120, fades rather than pops, and once gone stays gone — it was fouling the Scrapbook and the SMS toast. |
| v1.136 | **The test console's knock is now `lyre`.** "hermes" was a bad word to type: h opened help, r read, m cycled the music, so spelling it set half the game off. Only three letters in the whole alphabet are unbound, too few to spell with — so the trigger now only needs to *begin* with a free letter, and from the first keypress every key is swallowed in capture phase. A mistyped `l` costs nothing. The **Scrapbook** is tabbed — ALL / PAPERS / SCIENCE / CODE / RON / SECRETS / CRAFT, drawn as paper index cards with live counts — so two hundred clippings stop being one endless scroll; arrows step between drawers. The tab clicks had to be handled before the player's click-to-swing, which runs earlier in the frame and ate the press: whoever runs earliest owns the input. |
| v1.135 | **Row out into the fog before you choose a heading.** You no longer pick a course off a menu while standing on the sand. On a seaworthy launch the boat pulls away, the land slides astern, the fog gathers ahead, and the chart opens from **open water** — a man alone on the sea deciding which way to point the bow, which is a different thing from a level select. Cancel and you come about and row home, the hull put back where it was drawn up. The chart itself is now a chart: aged paper, sea ink, serif type, a true Greek-key meander tiled top and bottom — the same motif as the marble columns and the death certificate. Each landfall carries its Homeric epithet, so the list reads as a rhapsode's catalogue of harbours: **OGYGIA** the navel of the sea, **AEGILIA** the goat isle harbourless, **AEAEA** where the dawn has her dancing-floor, **THRINACIA** the island of the Sun, **ITHACA** clear-seen, a good nurse of young men. |
| v1.134 | **Bug fix: the Scrapbook scrolls again — the camera zoom was eating the wheel.** Not a focus problem at all. The main loop consumed the mouse wheel for camera zoom unconditionally, a couple of hundred lines above the pass that ticks the Scrapbook, so by the time the panel asked for the wheel the queue had already been drained to zero, every frame. Each attempt to read silently zoomed the world behind the page instead. The zoom now yields while the archive is open. |
| v1.133 | **A test console: type `hermes` in-game.** A development tool for exercising the code without playing to it — typed as a word rather than bound to a key, so it cannot be found by accident, with the listener in capture phase. Buttons for the two things wanted constantly: jump to any island (all five plus the Backspace, routed through the real world registry so far islands build lazily exactly as they do when you sail there), and kits — AI key, Trojan card, Hermes card armed against all four daemons, chip and manual, golden axe, ship parts, weapons, backpack and maps. Plus a command line: `give`, `items`, `arm`, `open`, `tp`, `heal`, `kill`, `where`. Nothing here reimplements game logic, so what you test is what ships. |
| v1.132 | **Poseidon's fog on the failed crossing.** Fog now closes round the boat during the turn-back. It does two jobs at once — it sells the refusal, the sea shutting in and walking you home, and it veils a lot of empty water at exactly the moment the camera is furthest from anything worth looking at. The weather follows the boat's own arc: rolling in behind you as the island falls away, closing hard on the crest while the sea takes hold, opening again as home comes back up. The banks drift in from the seaward heading — the way you were trying to go — so the motion reads as refusal rather than as weather. The trick worth noting: round blobs, however many, average into a flat wash and read as haze; the banks are squashed hard along their own orbit tangent, and elongated shelves shearing past one another are what the eye picks up as rotation. |
| v1.131 | **Every island gets one unmistakable landform.** You should know where you are from the *shape* of the ground, not only its colour. **AEGILIA** a fire scar of dead standing trunks on scorched dirt, one of the few places on the goat isle with clear sightlines; **AEAEA** a reed fen of stippled pools through tallgrass, slow to cross and easy to lose a sightline in — a witch's ground; **THRINACIA** the dust-bowl, a great dished crater of sand you walk down into; **ITHACA** an olive grove in ordered rows, the only regular planting in the archipelago, because someone once looked after it. **OGYGIA** keeps the lotus grove, now hers alone. Ithaca's beauty pass widens that grove to 214 trees and gives home 390 wildflowers — more than double any other island — while the burnt rock and the scorched pasture are scaled down, so the bloom order reads as a moral. **Cars are retired** from every island (the sprites, the salvage and the inspection are kept whole, ready for a better vehicle). |
| v1.130 | **The islands stop being one map.** World-building took only a seed, so all five islands were the *same* map with a different random stream: river at x=40, identical road grid, the same thirteen building lots, the same hills. It now takes a terrain profile — river position, width, meander and axis (or none at all), road layout, lot count, hills, hollows, forest density, meadows — and each island declares its own character. **OGYGIA** keeps the reference layout, and is now the only island with the lotus grove; **AEGILIA** a torrent cutting the east, no town, the most mountainous ground of the five; **AEAEA** no roads at all, the heaviest forest cover (1142 trees against Ogygia's 768), a broad slow river running east–west; **THRINACIA** no river whatsoever, sparsest woods, wide open pasture and one coastal road — nowhere to hide from a sun that is also the sensor; **ITHACA** every feature at its kindest, and the abundance is the reward. |
| v1.129 | **Each island gets its own ground.** Every island rendered in identical greens because the floor table is a shared singleton read straight by the renderer — mutating it repaints all five at once. An island now hangs its own palette and foliage tint, which the floors, the minimap and the trees prefer over the defaults. **OGYGIA** lush cool green and pale shell sand, a comfortable prison; **AEGILIA** burnt volcanic rock and ash; **AEAEA** dark over-fed green, where everything grows too well; **THRINACIA** sun-bleached gold, parched; **ITHACA** warm olive and meadow. The fortress decks are deliberately *not* overridden — the machine architecture is the one thing identical wherever you land, and it should read as imported, not native. Foliage uses a pre-tinted copy of the tree sheet cached per tint, since tinting hundreds of trees a frame would cost far too much. |
| v1.128 | **M4 scouts form up and sweep as a search team.** When they lose you they no longer hunt as a scatter of individuals: up to four take an arrowhead — point leading, wings out, tail trailing — which reads as a deliberate hunt and puts four sightlines abreast instead of one. Keeping them off each other took more than spacing (a bump costs both machines 2 HP and an M4 has only 16; the first cut killed two scouts in a minute of testing): each scout steers away from a fellow inside its personal bubble *and* eases the throttle as it closes, because steering alone cannot win when two machines close head-on inside a frame. The formation's heading is rate-limited — a snapped heading teleports the wing slots across each other, which is how formation flyers collide — and each scout gets its own spiral phase, since two working the same last contact otherwise traced the same spiral into each other. The search itself was broken too: it was time-driven, so its target swept the circle faster than a scout could walk and simply dragged the machine in a tight circle at the centre. It is waypoint-driven now, advancing only when a leg is walked, and genuinely expands outward. |
| v1.127 | **Bug fix: fortress guards were going inert.** Three separate faults made them lose their point and hang about. **Battery** — guards ran the overworld scavengers' economy, so a guard chasing you hit its low threshold after about 44 seconds, dropped aggro, walked back to its muster point and sat there recharging *and* healing to full. Fortress guards now draw off the mains: they never drain, never break off, and a wounded one no longer limps home. **Give-up timers** — an M4 forgot you after 9 seconds without sight, an M5 or M6 after 20. They now stay hostile once they have acquired you; only destroying one, a terminal stun, or the island's mind dying takes one off you. To keep the alarm workable, "is hunting" and "has eyes on you right now" are separate, so breaking line of sight still quiets the fortress and stops the reinforcement waves without making the guards forget. **The freeze** — on reaching your last-seen tile with nobody there, an M4 did literally nothing and stood on the spot forever; it now sweeps a widening spiral. |
| v1.126 | **Per-island virus codes: one card no longer opens the archipelago.** Holding a Trojan card was the only credential check, so a card forged on Ogygia opened every fortress forever. Each island's HERMES relay now holds its own daemon's payload, drawn from what undoes them in Homer — **zeus-virus** for CALYPSO (the sky-father's command), **nobody-virus** for POLYPHEMUS (Outis, the name the eye cannot repeat), **moly-virus** for CIRCE (Hermes' herb, warding the record), **eclipse-virus** for HELIOS (the sun blotted from heaven). Arming happens per island: forge that island's payload at its own relay and copy it onto the card; naming another island's code is refused. The gates escalate — the Lion's Gate takes any Trojan card, so entry stays quick, but a new **sanctum door** across the maze's exit corridor reads the card for *this* island's virus, and a card armed elsewhere walks the whole labyrinth to be stopped there. The core itself is shielded: melee, bombs and the electro-arc all turn until the island's virus is run at its core terminal. The arming lives on the card, so losing it costs the arming until you reprint and reforge. |
| v1.125 | **Garrison the labyrinth.** The maze was an empty walk — two light scouts on the quad and nothing in the corridors, so the fortress fell before the alarm ever tripped. A standing watch now sits inside the maze band: **M6** packs patrolling the corridors and **M5** snipers holding the deep straights nearer the sanctum, each with a post to snipe from instead of chasing. Guards are spread and never seated in the gate mouth, so stepping through the door is not an instant ambush (nearest about 19 tiles in). They spawn unaggroed on purpose — the M-classes acquire by genuine sight only, so the maze becomes a stealth problem and a careful raider can still ghost it. Polyphemus takes the heaviest watch at 7 + 4; Circe and Helios 5 + 3. Calypso is exempt: her guards detain rather than kill. |
| v1.124 | **Calypso's sniper fires a bolt you can dodge.** In depart mode the **M5** no longer hitscans an instant orange laser — it launches a soporific bolt at roughly a third of the war-laser's speed, about a second and a half to cross eight tiles, flying to where you *stood* when it fired. Step aside and it lands on empty ground with a soft puff and never counts as a detain strike. Drawn in Calypso indigo as a fat, haloed orb rather than a thin dart, so its slowness and its invitation read at a glance. Kill-mode snipers keep their instant laser unchanged. |
| v1.123 | **Depart mode — the daemon you leave, not the one you kill.** Calypso's core is indestructible: a wrecking tool meets a refusal in her own register, bombs and the electro-arc cannot raze her, and the kill-hook never fires. The way out is the sea. Refunctioning her is her fall, so it now records CALYPSO in the daemons-down tally, since the core-kill that tallies the martial daemons never happens here. Her fortress guards **detain** rather than kill: the first three strikes dose you with lotus torpor and shove you back toward the island's heart — *kalyptō*, kept not killed — and only sustained intrusion past that wounds, with the count resetting once you are off her guards' radar. Only her fortress guards detain; Poseidon's roaming machines stay lethal and the kill islands are untouched. |
| v1.122 | Phone handling: clicking anywhere off the LCD puts it away — the backdrop, the handset's own body, even the sprite's transparent margins, which used to swallow the click without closing; an **X** on the status row beside the battery gives touch a reliable way out where there is no Esc; and **O** opens the phone from anywhere (and closes it, unless you are typing in the thread). |
| v1.121 | **Back to the whole handset.** The phone modal returns to the v1.118 framing — earpiece and badge above, navi key below, keypad falling off the bottom, no dimming — the shot where the phone reads as a phone. | v1.120 | The LCD overlay is nudged to cover the sprite's screen window fully — the earlier pixel-measured box under-read the shadowed glass edges and left a sliver of the sprite's own screen showing — and the handset behind it renders dimmed, so the body recedes, any residual seam disappears, and only the lit screen draws the eye. |
| v1.119 | The phone modal zooms all the way into the glass: the screen fills about 70% of the viewport width, framed only by the bezel and the navi-key arc, with the maker's badge out of frame. | v1.117 | **The handset is the modal: texts and Snake live on the 3310's own screen.** No more blown-up LCD panel beside the phone. | v1.116 | **Snake on the 3310, and you wash ashore.** The real thing on an 84×48 canvas at authentic LCD resolution, fat pixels and all: arrows or WASD steer (captured before game input), a tap on the left or right half turns on touch, the edges wrap, seven is a feed, and the high score persists with your save. **Click-to-eat**: tapping food in a pocket, pack or sleeve eats one of it, because mobile has no eat key and you could starve holding a tin. New lore: *Field Note: the Old Handsets*, the DCT3 silicon history as the reason the one phone that still serves is the oldest one. |
| v1.115 | **The Nokia 3310 gets a dashboard slot.** A **PHONE** box beside the walkman shows the handset with live signal bars — stronger the closer you stand to Calypso's core, no signal off her island at all. Click it for a full-screen modal: handset art and a pea-green LCD with **CALYPSO** and **RON** tabs, scrollable threads and an input row. She answers by hold band (warm, wary, cold) on stay, leave, robots, love and Poseidon; **RON** answers in a clipped lowercase radio voice. Texting her nudges her hold up, and her ambient warnings file into the thread rather than vanishing. No signal means the message is not sent. |
| v1.114 | Two small fixes: the **Armoury** was drawing an undiscovered weapon's power bar filled and printing its rating, leaking the strength of something you have not found and contradicting its own "??? undiscovered" label — an undiscovered row now shows an empty track and "pwr ?". | v1.113 | **Bug fix: the Scrapbook scrolls again.** Its key handler swallowed *every* key while open, to keep arrows and WASD out of player movement — but the blanket suppression also killed the native scrolling the help promises, so long entries were unreadable past the first page. | v1.112 | **Calypso texts you.** She was never your enemy. The machines roaming Ogygia are **POSEIDON**'s; Calypso is the keeper caught between wanting you to stay and not wanting his things to kill you — a dead guest cannot be kept. So she texts you, on a Nokia 3310 you land with: warnings about his robots, tips, and always a reason to stay. The help *is* the trap. Her **hold** on you (0 to 1, starting at 0.65) is her protection of you: it drifts up while you linger inland, down beside a beached vessel, and steps down on every leaving-signal — each ship part −0.05, a boat −0.15, the golden axe −0.25, the finished ship −0.20, boarding −0.30 — each tied to the text that marks it. When Poseidon throws you back from a failed crossing it *rises* +0.15, the two gods and one leash, and she texts her relief. While her hold is not cold she will freeze one of his machines bearing down on you, in her own indigo rather than the amber of a hack; below cold she does nothing, so you lose her exactly when you want her. Fourteen triggers, one-shots recorded so a reload does not re-tutorial, and the real 3310 tone: Morse for "SMS". |
| v1.111 | **The Backspace's ways up become labelled doors, one per island.** The alternative crossing road was a menu; it is diegetic now. The pocket is littered with one **EXIT** door per island, each set into a different room's west wall so its sign reads square-on to the isometric camera, with the island's name burning on the green sign — OGYGIA, AEGILIA, AEAEA, THRINACIA, ITHACA. Walk into the door you want and you come up on that island. The doors *are* the choice; no chart. |
| v1.110 | **HELIOS (Thrinacia) is built — the roster is complete and the campaign finishable.** The island that does not hunt you: the cattle of the Sun graze the headland, gold-haloed, tame, and forbidden.
