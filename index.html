Books

Some buildings still hold books. Read one (R) and the knowledge is yours for good; not even death takes it from you. Woodcraft, herb lore, tracking, endurance: each book teaches a skill that changes how you survive.

Backpack

A rare find in the ruins. Once you have one it carries itself: 16 more slots and a spare-weapon slot, filled automatically once your pockets are full. Eating (Q) and firing a gun draw from it too, once your pockets run dry — no need to shuffle things forward. Press I to see what's in it. Die, and you drop it, and everything in it, right where you fell.

Staying alive

Food drains all the time and faster when you sprint. Find tinned food in the broken buildings and berries in the tall grass, and walk over loot to pocket it. Health only recovers when you are well fed; starve and it drains away. Trees felled with your penknife drop wood. Nights are dark and dangerous: find torches in the buildings to carry a pool of light with you.

The animals

Feral dogsHunt in packs; barking (!) means they have seen you. Hurt one and the pack backs off. Don't let them surround you.
BoarsPaw the ground, then charge dead straight. Step aside: a boar that hits a wall stuns itself.
VipersNear-invisible in tall grass; their venom drains health long after the bite. Where there are berries, there are snakes.
RavensThey don't bite; they tell everything else where you are, and they steal food. Keep to the trees.

The machines

The AIs still control this land. Their black obelisk towers pulse across the landscape, and their T-class hunters patrol around them.

T1A wheeled hunter, faster than your walk. It cannot climb: put any rise between you, or lure it into a hollow and it is trapped for good. Sprinting outruns it.
T2A biped stalker that walks exactly at your pace and does not tire. Sprint to break away, or stand and fight.

Your penknife barely scratches them. RON — a resistance calling itself Reality or Nothing, whether or not it's still out there — hid weapons in crates inside the buildings: search them (E) for crowbars, bats, machetes, and if you are lucky the guns: a stun-gun (drops a machine cold for a while), an electro-gun (fuses one into a wreck you can mine for scrap), a pistol or a shotgun (holes in anything, but gunfire ruins the salvage). Guns are dead weight without batteries, ammo, or shells, hidden in the same crates.

Every machine runs on a battery: hunting drains it and they crawl home to their obelisk to recharge. One that gets stuck runs flat where it stands, and a flat machine can be reprogrammed (R, costs a battery). It turns friendly, follows you, and a friendly T2 fells trees for you.

The obelisks can be brought down. Carry a stun-gun, an electro-gun and a Wi-Fi block together and you're prompted to build an OB-gun (C): five burns topple a tower into a heap of circuit boards, batteries and scrap. A rare insane bomb can level one on its own. Every toppled tower drops numbered circuit boards — collect all eight and you can build a wave gun (C), which fans laser fire through a whole crowd at once. Bring every obelisk down before the SKYLINK countdown reaches zero, or the machines' single waking mind comes online and it's over.

Out on the water, W2 droids hover in squads just above the river. Idle, they drift; but come to the bank near them and the whole squad turns and fires on you in waves.

Bombs

Scavenged timed bombs come in four sizes — small, medium, large, and a rare insane one. Hold a bomb and use it (/ or left click) to set it ticking a step ahead of you; then get clear. When the fuse runs out it goes off in a cloud of fire that hurts everything nearby — animals, machines, droids, and you. The insane bomb is fierce enough to bring down an obelisk.

The world

A river divides the map; the bridges are the only crossings. Streams can be waded, slowly. High ground costs stamina to climb. Somewhere in the ruins, the story of what happened here is waiting to be found. Sprayed slogans turn up here and there on the walls — some anti-machine, some signed by RON — and abandoned cars sit dead on the roads where the grid failed them.
